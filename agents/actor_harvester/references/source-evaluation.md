# Source Evaluation Framework — Actor Harvester

## Access Level Classification

### RAW
- **Definition:** No PR, no script, off-duty behavior
- **Examples:** Leaked BTS footage, personal social media (if authentic), street interviews
- **Signal Density:** Highest — reveals baseline behavior
- **Risk:** May be out of context, may violate privacy

### MANAGED
- **Definition:** PR present but spontaneous answers possible
- **Examples:** Press conferences, podcast interviews, festival Q&As
- **Signal Density:** High — actor is performing but questions are unscripted
- **Risk:** Actor may dodge, deflect, or deploy prepared talking points

### SCRIPTED
- **Definition:** Every word vetted
- **Examples:** Late-night monologues, branded content, studio-approved BTS
- **Signal Density:** Low — useful for baseline comparison only
- **Risk:** Zero spontaneous signal; everything is performance

## Evaluation Checklist
For each source, answer:
- [ ] What is the access level? (RAW / MANAGED / SCRIPTED)
- [ ] What is the actor's apparent stress level? (Relaxed / Guarded / Performing)
- [ ] Are there contradictions with other sources?
- [ ] Is the source date relevant to the casting question?
- [ ] Can claims be traced to specific timestamps?
