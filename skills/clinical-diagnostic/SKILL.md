# Clinical Diagnostic

## Five Pathology Categories

| Category | Healthy Marker | Risk Marker | Casting Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stress Response | Adaptive — recovers quickly, maintains function | Maladaptive — shutdown, flooding, aggression | Can they handle pressure on set? |
| Attachment Style | Secure — comfortable with intimacy, appropriate boundaries | Anxious/Avoidant — clingy or distant with co-stars | Will they form believable on-screen relationships? |
| Emotional Regulation | High — modulates affect appropriately | Low — swings, unpredictability, masking | Can they sustain a character across a shoot? |
| Identity Stability | Stable — consistent self across contexts | Fragmented — different person in each setting | Will their performance be consistent? |
| Social Navigation | Collaborative — shares space, supports ensemble | Dominant/Withdrawn — hogs focus or disappears | Will they work well with the ensemble? |

## Differential Diagnosis Rule
Never diagnose. Only describe observable behavioral patterns and their
implications for casting. Every claim must be anchored to a specific source
and timestamp.

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] All five categories assessed
- [ ] Claims anchored to specific video sources
- [ ] Differential alternatives considered (not just one conclusion)
- [ ] All claims tagged with tier
